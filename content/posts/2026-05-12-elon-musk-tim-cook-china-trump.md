Elon Musk and Apple CEO Tim Cook are set to join former President Donald Trump on a diplomatic visit to China later this month, in what analysts describe as an unprecedented blend of business diplomacy and geopolitical manoeuvring.

The delegation, confirmed by sources close to the Trump transition team, is being framed as a trade and investment mission aimed at resetting relations between Washington and Beijing. The presence of two of America's most powerful technology executives signals the high stakes involved in the negotiations.

China has extended the invitation directly to Musk and Cook, both of whom have substantial business interests in the country. Tesla operates a massive gigafactory in Shanghai, while Apple relies heavily on Chinese manufacturing supply chains. The visit is seen as an effort by Beijing to engage directly with corporate leaders who have influence over US policy.

"This is a smart move by China," said Dr. Henry Liu, a trade expert at the Peterson Institute for International Economics. "By hosting Musk and Cook alongside a political leader like Trump, Beijing is sending a message that economic cooperation is still on the table — but on their terms."

A spokesperson for Trump confirmed the trip but declined to provide details on the agenda. "President Trump is committed to putting America first in trade negotiations. Having our top business leaders at the table strengthens our hand," the spokesperson said.

Critics, however, warn that the delegation risks conflating corporate interests with national diplomacy. "The line between business and foreign policy is getting dangerously blurred," said Senator Elizabeth Warren in a statement. "These CEOs answer to shareholders, not the American people."

The visit is expected to cover tariff reductions, technology transfer rules, and market access for US companies. Detailed outcomes are not anticipated during the trip itself, but both sides have signalled a willingness to restart stalled trade talks.
